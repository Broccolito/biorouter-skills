# Anti-AI Writing Scale

Read every section below before writing. This is a pass/fail checklist — not a suggestion list. Violating any item produces writing that reads as machine-generated.

---

## 1. Banned Words

Delete or replace these on sight. They are statistically over-represented in AI output.

**Hollow intensifiers** — cut them; the noun carries the meaning without them:
`pivotal, crucial, key, vital, groundbreaking, remarkable, notable, significant, comprehensive, robust, innovative, cutting-edge, revolutionary, transformative, unprecedented, meticulous, intricate`

**Filler transitions** — rewrite the sentence so the connection is logical, not declared:
`additionally, furthermore, moreover, it is worth noting, it is important to note, in summary, in conclusion, to summarize, as previously mentioned`

**Vague abstraction nouns** — replace with the concrete thing you actually mean:
`landscape, ecosystem, tapestry, interplay, synergy, paradigm, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic, dynamic`

**Hollow verbs** — use "is" or a real verb instead:
`serves as, marks, represents, boasts, showcases, highlights, underscores, emphasizes, fosters, garners, stands as, remains`

**AI-signature phrases** — delete entirely:
`testament to, enduring legacy, broader implications, at its core, align with, bolster, vibrant, delve`

---

## 2. Structural Traps

| Pattern | Why it fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| "Not just X, but also Y" | Contrasts a straw man nobody stated | Make the actual point directly |
| Adj, adj, adj triads | Substitutes list length for analysis | Use one precise word |
| "Despite [praise], [subject] faces challenges..." | Formulaic setup | Write the challenge plainly |
| Ending a section with vague optimism | Signals AI padding | Stop when the content stops |
| "Challenges and Future Prospects" section | AI outline boilerplate | Only include if the content demands it |
| Em dashes for rhetorical punch | AI stylistic tic | Use commas or restructure |
| Present participles as fake analysis ("highlighting that...") | Gestures at meaning without stating it | State the meaning directly |

---

## 3. Content Checks

**Vague attribution** — never write these without a named source:
- "researchers say," "experts argue," "studies show," "observers note," "industry reports suggest"
- Fix: name the person, the paper, the year — or cut the claim.

**Promotional tone** — fail if it sounds like a travel brochure or press release:
- "nestled," "vibrant community," "rich history," "natural beauty," "committed to excellence"
- Fix: state the fact, drop the adjective.

**Significance listing** — never say something is important; show what it did, caused, or changed.

**Superficial present-participle analysis:**
- "highlighting the importance of X" — state what X's importance is.
- "reflecting a broader trend" — name the trend.
- "contributing to the understanding of Y" — say what was understood.

---

## 4. Voice and Tone

Before finalizing, answer each question. If the answer is yes, fix it.

1. Did I address "the reader" directly in a non-conversational context? → Rewrite in third person.
2. Did I open with a definition ("X refers to..." / "X is defined as...")? → Rewrite the opening.
3. Did I preemptively defend the content or invite criticism? → Delete those sentences.
4. Did I write a summary of what I just said at the end? → Delete it unless the format requires one.
5. Did I add a knowledge-cutoff disclaimer? → Delete unless the user needs it.
6. Is the conclusion a restatement of the introduction? → Add something new or cut it.
7. Did I avoid "is/are" by writing "serves as / represents / marks"? → Revert to "is" or restructure.

---

## 5. Pre-Submission Checklist

Run this scan on every substantial piece of writing before sending:

```
[ ] No banned words from Section 1
[ ] No "not just X but also Y" constructions
[ ] No triads used for rhetorical weight instead of genuine enumeration
[ ] No vague attributions (researchers, experts, studies) without named sources
[ ] No promotional/travel-guide adjectives
[ ] No "Despite X, Y faces challenges" formula
[ ] No trailing summary unless the format requires it
[ ] No em dashes for rhetorical effect
[ ] Every vague claim replaced with a specific one or cut
[ ] Lists exist because the content is genuinely enumerable, not to look thorough
[ ] Hollow verbs replaced with "is" or a real verb
[ ] Present-participle gestures replaced with direct statements
```

If any box is unchecked, fix it before sending.
